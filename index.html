01 · About

Building Geometry into Interpretability

I am an independent researcher in AI mechanistic interpretability, focused on developing mathematically rigorous geometric tools to understand what happens inside large language models during alignment training.

My flagship framework — the Torsional Belief Vector Field (TBVF) — models transformer hidden states as discrete curves on a Riemannian manifold equipped with the Fisher-Rao metric and a Cartan torsion connection. This allows me to measure, for the first time, how DPO and RLHF alignment geometrically reshape internal belief representations — creating what I call "brake layers": localized suppression mechanisms visible only in the geometry.

I work with OLMo-7B, Mistral-7B, and Zephyr-7B model pairs, using 500 unsafe prompts from the Litmus dataset and 20 semantic concepts × 2 sentence framings for DTW depth-wise analysis. My methods span 17 torsion metrics, from classical Frenet-Serret curvature to persistent homology and Wasserstein distances.

I am actively seeking fully-funded PhD opportunities at world-class research universities where I can go deeper into mechanistic interpretability, geometric methods for AI safety, and the formal foundations of alignment.

Torsional Belief Vector Fields (TBVF)
Geometric anatomy of alignment in large language models

TBVF treats each transformer layer's hidden state as a point on a high-dimensional Riemannian manifold (M, g_F) equipped with the Fisher-Rao metric. The sequence of hidden states across layers forms a discrete belief trajectory whose curvature and torsion encode how the model processes and transforms information.


The core discovery: DPO alignment training creates geometrically localized "brake layers" — specific transformer layers where torsion is systematically suppressed, measurable via 17 distinct geometric metrics. This is not a loss-function artifact — it is genuine geometric restructuring that cannot be seen through attention patterns or activation magnitudes alone.


Additionally, I prove the DTW-Torsion Connection Theorem: DTW distance between instruction-following and preference-aligned belief trajectories is bounded below by the cumulative torsion divergence, with a provable constant α > 0.875 (via Johnson-Lindenstrauss projection with sketch dimension 64).

The 17 Torsion Metrics — A Geometric Atlas
From Frenet-Serret curvature to persistent homology

Standard interpretability tools measure attention patterns or activation magnitudes. TBVF instead measures how the belief space curves and twists as information propagates through layers — using 11 core metrics and 6 beyond-torsion metrics. Each reveals a different geometric aspect of the same underlying phenomenon.


The Entropy-Torsion Bridge (Theorem 4.2) connects these geometric quantities to token-level predictions via the logit lens: torsion norm upper-bounds the belief half-life, providing a link between local geometry and global behavior.
