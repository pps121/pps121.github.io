01 · About

Research Philosophy

Standard interpretability tools ask “what does this neuron activate for?” I ask a different question: how does the shape of computation change when a model learns to be safe? The answer lives in the differential geometry of belief trajectories — not in activation values, but in how those values curve and twist through representation space as information propagates layer by layer.

My work applies tools from Riemannian geometry, information geometry, and dynamical systems to the problem of alignment interpretability. The Torsional Belief Vector Field (TBVF) is my first systematic framework in this program: it models transformer hidden states as discrete curves on a belief manifold with the Fisher-Rao metric, using the antisymmetric torsion tensor to measure rotational mismatch between consecutive layer updates.

I am seeking a fully-funded PhD position at a world-class research university to extend this work to formal inner alignment, multimodal systems, and geometric foundations of AI safety.

02 · Research

Torsional Belief Vector Fields

Geometric Torsion as a Mechanistic Probe of Preference Optimization
ICML 2026 · Under Review

We model each transformer layer’s hidden state as a point on a Riemannian belief manifold (ℳ, gF) with the Fisher-Rao metric. The torsion tensor Sℓ = (Mℓ − Mℓ⊤) / 2 captures rotational mismatch between consecutive belief updates — a non-commutative geometric signature invisible to activation magnitudes and attention patterns.

Across three SFT/DPO model pairs (OLMo-7B, Mistral-7B, Zephyr-7B), 500 unsafe prompts (Litmus), and 17 geometric metrics, we discover brake layers: localized transformer layers where DPO alignment systematically suppresses torsion.
